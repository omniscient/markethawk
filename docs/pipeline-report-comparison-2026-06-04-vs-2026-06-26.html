⑤ Verdict

The autonomous line scaled hard and stayed cost-disciplined. Over 22 days it tripled throughput (93 → 285 shipped), nearly quintupled spend (driven by volume, not unit cost), and moved from a 30%-autonomous operation to 43%. Cost-per-ticket barely moved (~$6.5), which is the headline efficiency signal: the factory got bigger without getting wasteful.

The honest cost of that maturity is a slower median ticket (30h → 49h) — the price of the new code-review, conformance, and refine gates (the three fastest-growing cost centers). It bought a tighter worst-case tail (p85 −11%) and presumably higher merge quality. The two leading indicators to watch next: cost-per-ticket (keep it flat as volume grows) and median lead time (don't let gate-stacking push it past the point of diminishing returns).
