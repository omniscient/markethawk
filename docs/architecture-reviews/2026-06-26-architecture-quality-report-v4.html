Overall Health

MarketHawk is a mature single-tenant full-stack stock-scanning + paper/live-trading platform at a high plateau. In the 17 days since v3 it completed the Data Quality Trust Gate epic (#491, #498–#502) — a versioned quality_gate.py/quality_gate_evidence.py pair enforcing a survivorship + backtesting contract before a signal can arm a trade — closed out the defensive-security remediation epic (#372) including live-IBKR hard guards (#368) with a Redis-backed fail-closed kill switch and non-bypassable position caps (providers/ibkr_orders.py:233-280), and — the standout — turned all three v3 “honesty gaps” into real artifacts: automated DB backups with a weekly restore-drill sidecar (R01/#195, the item that shipped empty in v3), ruff now gating in CI (R02), and a deep readiness probe + migration-gate ADR-0012 (R05). A factory-platform outage (Python 3.14/scipy) was diagnosed and fixed in the same window (#622). Overall quality rose 90→91; the weighted scorecard 4.06→4.12; the single dimension to move was Operational Readiness 3→4, breaking a three-review plateau.

The character of the risk has shifted once more. v1 was missing foundations; v2 was unfinished edges; v3 was process honesty at autonomous speed; v4's risk is a mature single-tenant ceiling. What remains is no longer missing foundations but structural gaps that cap several dimensions at once and none of which is a defect today: there is no authorization model (single-user, RBAC pending #373 — now the #1 ceiling), the platform is single-replica with in-memory WebSocket connection counters (core/ws_limits.py:18-19) and no read replicas, and maintainability debt is quietly compounding — UTC-normalization duplication grew to 287 sites (still no utils/time.py) and the live-trading entrypoint maybe_execute() reached a 290-line, 15-branch guard cascade as #368’s safety checks piled onto an already-large function. These are ceilings on multi-tenant and horizontal growth, not cracks in the current product.

Top 5 Strengths (new since v3)

  1. Automated, verified backups + restore-drill — the three-review-old existential data-loss risk is closed with real artifacts: a db-backup sidecar plus a weekly db-restore-drill that proves the dump restores (docker-compose.yml:30-72, :368-407). This single change moved Operational Readiness 3→4 and Backup/Recovery 1→5.
  2. Live-IBKR fail-closed kill switch — a Redis-backed kill switch plus non-bypassable position/notional caps gate every live order (#368, providers/ibkr_orders.py:233-280), survived two adversarial reviews, and raise the security posture without a single regression.
  3. Complete Data Quality Trust Gate — quality_gate.py + quality_gate_evidence.py implement a schema-versioned survivorship + backtesting contract with lineage (#491/#498–#502), shipped with tests; a clean module split rather than another god file.
  4. v3 honesty gaps closed in CI — ruff check now runs as a blocking CI step (R02 resolved), a deep /ready probe checks DB/Redis (routers/health.py:34), and migrations are gated by ADR-0012 — the “documented gate that didn’t gate” problem from v3 is gone.
  5. Factory dependency modernization — the Python-3.14/scipy platform outage was root-caused and the scientific stack bumped to cp314 wheels (#622, requirements.txt), keeping the autonomous pipeline itself buildable.

Top 5 Risks

  1. MEDIUM–HIGH No authorization model — the platform is single-user with no RBAC; authorization is authenticated-vs-not only (§7 Authz = 2/5). Split out as epic #373 with a design still pending. This is now the single largest ceiling on any multi-tenant trajectory. (carried)
  2. MEDIUM Scalability is single-instance, four reviews running — in-memory WebSocket connection counters (core/ws_limits.py:18-19) are not multi-replica-safe and there are no read replicas; the API/DB tier cannot scale horizontally. (carried, structurally stuck)
  3. MEDIUM maybe_execute() guard cascade — the live-trading entrypoint is now 290 lines / ~15 branches after #368 piled safety checks onto an already-large function (auto_trade_service.py:92-381); a testability and maintainability hazard exactly where money moves. (R01v4)
  4. MEDIUM UTC-normalization duplication grew to 287 sites — v3’s R03 was never addressed; the .astimezone(timezone.utc).replace(tzinfo=None) idiom now appears 287 times with still no utils/time.py owner. (R03v4)
  5. LOW Frontend / API god files undecomposed four reviews — api/scanner.ts (787) and QualityReportModal.tsx (753) are byte-for-byte unchanged across v1–v4. (carried, R10 v3)

Top 5 Recommended Actions

  1. Resolve the authorization question — implement #373 RBAC or write an ADR formally accepting the single-user ceiling — Effort: L, closes the #1 carried ceiling.
  2. Decompose maybe_execute() into extracted _validate_* decision steps — Effort: M, closes R01v4.
  3. Create app/utils/time.py (utc_now(), to_utc_naive()) and codemod the 287 call sites — Effort: M, closes R03v4 (4 reviews old).
  4. Move WebSocket connection counters to Redis and add read replicas — Effort: M, unsticks Scalability.
  5. Extract a QualityGatePolicy interface so a new evidence type touches one module, not three — Effort: S, closes R04v4.

3. Architecture Assessment

3.1 Separation of Concerns

Score: 4/5 Good Δ 0

Change: Routers stayed thin; the new quality-gate logic lives in services, not routers, and the domain exception hierarchy (exceptions.py) continues to keep web types out of the core.

Finding (caps the score): services/universe_export.py:11-12,59 still raises HTTPException directly from the service layer — the one place a web type crosses the seam, and the reason the service can’t be unit-tested as a pure function. (R02v4)

3.2 Module Boundaries

Score: 4/5 Good Δ 0

Change: The quality-gate split (quality_gate.py + quality_gate_evidence.py) added clean new seams rather than another monolith; the facade surfaces from v3 hold.

Finding (caps the score): there are still no interfaces beyond providers/base.py, lazy-import cycles persist, and coupling grew — quality_gate_evidence reaches into the SplitAdjustment model, so a new evidence type touches three modules. (R04v4)

3.3 Dependency Direction

Score: 3/5 Acceptable Δ 0

Change: No new cycles; flow still inward; the registry remains populated by side-effect importlib imports and string-named beat tasks.

Finding (caps the score): dispatch is static-method based throughout (ScannerService, QualityGate) and therefore not DI-injectable — tests can’t substitute a fake without monkeypatching, and the trust gate inherits the same rigidity.

3.4 Coupling and Cohesion

Score: 4/5 Good Δ 0

Change: The scanner decomposition from v3 holds and the quality-gate concern is its own cohesive pair of modules.

Finding (caps the score): auto_trade_service is hard-coupled to quality_gate, and there are three modules to touch per new evidence type (quality_gate.py + quality_gate_evidence.py + auto_trade_service.py) — orchestration coupling concentrating in the live-trading path. (R04v4)

3.5 API Design

Score: 5/5 Excellent Δ 0

Finding: No drift. Uniform /api/v1/ versioning, correct status codes, a schema-versioned quality gate, and sort allowlists on list endpoints. (The raw-JSONB exposure that pulls the §9 API Consistency sub-score to 4 is a data-shaping concern, not a convention violation; N+1 belongs to 3.6.)

3.6 Data Access Patterns

Score: 4/5 Good Δ 0

Finding (caps the score): eager loading remains confined to two selectinload uses, leaving N+1 margins on list paths; there are still no transaction context managers, so multi-write operations rely on implicit session-scope commits.

3.7 Error Handling Architecture

Score: 5/5 Excellent Δ 0

Finding: The typed domain hierarchy with deterministic error_id hashing and retryable semantics is intact; CircuitBreakerError still maps to a non-retryable provider error, and the new live-trading failures fold into the same hierarchy.

3.8 Configuration Management

Score: 5/5 Excellent Δ 0

Finding: Fail-fast validators reject weak JWT_SECRET_KEY (≥32) and REDIS_PASSWORD (≥16) and all required fields at startup; live-trading arming (LIVE_TRADING_ARMED) and the kill switch are typed, env-tunable settings. No raw os.getenv in app code.

3.9 Observability Architecture

Score: 5/5 Excellent Δ 0

Change: Metrics + tracing + logs + alerts remain wired end-to-end; this window added a live_orders_total counter and a LiveOrderPlaced Grafana alert (grafana/provisioning/alerting/rules.yaml).

Finding: alert routing is webhook-only (no email/Slack/PagerDuty), and OTLP trace export remains conditional with no metric exporter — the corner that keeps Distributed Tracing at 4/5 in the §6 table.

3.10 Security Architecture

Score: 4/5 Good Δ 0

Change: Epic #372 fully completed this window — secrets validators, CSRF, pre-accept WS auth, route-class rate limits, log redaction and Docker hardening — plus #368’s live-IBKR fail-closed guards raised the posture with zero regressions.

Finding (caps the score): there is still no authorization model (single-user, no RBAC — §7 Authz = 2/5, epic #373), and TLS remains an opt-in --profile tls path rather than the default.

3.11 Deployment Architecture

Score: 4/5 Good Δ 0

Change: The v3 deploy-path traps are closed — a deep readiness probe (routers/health.py:34), an alembic check migration gate (ADR-0012), an admin-env-check, and automated backups now exist in the compose path.

Finding (caps the score): TLS is still opt-in (--profile tls) and there is no automated CD — deploys remain a human-triggered docker compose rather than a pipeline.

8. Performance & Scalability

Resolved / improved since v3

  • Outbound latency bounded — provider connect/read timeouts are now configured everywhere (Timeout 3→5 in §6), so a slow Polygon/IBKR no longer blocks worker threads unbounded.
  • Pool observability — pool size 20+10 with pre_ping and exported gauges; live-order throughput now has a live_orders_total counter.

Still open

  • Sparse eager loading — only 2 selectinload uses across the service layer; list endpoints retain N+1 margins.
  • Sync ORM (ADR-0004, by design) — requests block threads on DB access.
  • Single API instance / single DB — in-memory WS connection counters (core/ws_limits.py:18-19) and no read replicas.

Scalability model: unchanged from v3 — Celery workers scale horizontally; the API tier is single-instance with WebSocket affinity unsolved and one Postgres with no replicas. Adequate for the single-user design point; the in-memory WS counters are the concrete blocker to running more than one API replica. This is the dimension structurally stuck at 3 for four reviews (§13).

18. Final Recommendations

Most important decision to make next

Decide whether MarketHawk is single-tenant forever. The absence of an authorization model is now the single largest ceiling — it caps Security Authz at 2/5 and is the reason Scalability has been stuck at 3 for four reviews. Either commit to #373 (RBAC, multi-user, the work that unsticks two dimensions) or write the ADR that formally accepts the single-user design point and re-scopes the rubric’s expectation. Leaving it implicit is the only thing keeping these dimensions ambiguous rather than honestly scored.

Most important practice to improve next

Reverse the duplication drift before it compounds. The one ledger moving the wrong way is duplication: UTC normalization grew 96→287, Redis-JSON to 195, and the money-path function maybe_execute swelled to 290 lines as guards accreted. None is a bug today, but each is borrowed maintainability that the autonomous pipeline will keep paying interest on. A utils/time.py codemod and a maybe_execute decomposition are the highest-leverage hygiene moves available.

Most important testing gap to close next

Service→service integration tests for the money path, plus backend mypy. The new auto_trade_service→quality_gate→ibkr_orders chain is exactly where a defect costs real money, yet it is only covered unit-deep (no integration tests) and entirely untyped (no mypy). These two together are what hold Testability and Developer Experience flat at 4.

Most important operational risk to reduce next

The single-replica ceiling and webhook-only alerting. With backups now real, the next operational gap is that the platform cannot run a second API replica (in-memory WS counters) and its only alert channel is a webhook — no email/Slack/PagerDuty and no formal incident/on-call process. Move WS counters to Redis and add a second alert route; both are bounded-effort and remove the last single-points in the run-time story.

Conclusion

v4 closes the chapter v3 opened on process honesty: every one of v3’s “honesty gaps” — the fictional backups closure, the bypassed ruff gate, the missing readiness probe — shipped as real, verifiable artifacts this window, and Operational Readiness broke a three-review plateau as a result. Overall quality ticked 90→91, the weighted scorecard 4.06→4.12, and no dimension regressed.

The trajectory is now best described as a mature single-tenant ceiling. The platform has run out of cheap foundational wins; what remains are structural decisions, not bug fixes. The score will not climb much further on hygiene alone — the next point of overall quality is gated on the multi-user/RBAC decision (#373) and the scalability work behind it. The honest path forward is to make that decision explicitly: commit to multi-tenant and unstick Scalability and Authorization, or ADR the single-user ceiling and let the rubric reflect a platform that has, by design, reached the top of its intended envelope.
